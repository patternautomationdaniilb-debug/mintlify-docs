Pattern Automation believes in transparency about collection and use of data. This policy provides information about how and when Pattern Automation uses cookies for these purposes. Capitalized terms used in this policy but not defined have the meaning set forth in our Privacy Policy, which also includes additional details about the collection and use of information at Pattern Automation.

**What is a cookie?**

Cookies are small text files sent by us to your computer or mobile device, which enable Pattern Automation features and functionality. They are unique to your account or your browser. Session-based cookies last only while your browser is open and are automatically deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies last until you or your browser delete them or until they expire.

**Does Pattern Automation use cookies?**

Yes. Pattern Automation uses cookies and similar technologies like web beacons. Pattern Automation uses both session-based and persistent cookies. Pattern Automation sets and accesses cookies on the domains operated by Pattern Automation and its corporate affiliates (collectively, the “Sites”). In addition, some third-party services used by Neuro AI may use third-party cookies, such as Hcaptcha.

**How is Pattern Automation using cookies?**

Some cookies are associated with your account and personal information to remember that you are logged in and which workspaces you are logged into. Other cookies are not tied to your account but are unique and allow us to carry out analytics and customization, among other similar things.

Cookies can be used to recognize you when you visit a Site or use our Services, remember your preferences, and give you a personalized experience that is consistent with your settings. Cookies also make your interactions faster and more secure. Visit our Cookie Table Sample to learn more.

**Categories of use**

- **Authentication:** If you're signed into the Services, cookies help Pattern Automation show you the right information and personalize your experience.
- **Security:** Pattern Automation uses cookies to enable and support security features, and to help detect malicious activity.
- **Preferences, features, and services:** Cookies denote which language you prefer and what your communications preferences are. They can help fill out forms on our Sites more easily. They also provide you with features, insights, and customized content.
- \*\*Performance and Analytics: \*\*Cookies help Pattern Automation learn how well the Sites and Services perform. Pattern Automation also uses cookies to understand, improve, and research products, features, and services, including to create logs and record when you access our Sites and Services from different devices, such as your work computer or your mobile device.
- **Advertising & Personalisation**\
  These cookies (and similar technologies) help deliver more relevant ads, prevent repeated ads, and improve campaign effectiveness. They may store or reference advertising click identifiers to attribute conversions and optimise performance.

**What third-party cookies does Pattern Automation use?**

You can find a list of the third-party cookies that Pattern Automation uses on our sites along with other relevant information in the Cookie Table Sample. Pattern Automation does its best to keep this table updated, but please note that the number and names of cookies, pixels, and other technologies may change from time to time.

**Are there cookies used for advertising purposes?**

Yes. We use advertising & personalisation cookies to show more relevant ads and to measure campaign performance.\
– EEA/UK/CH: we set these cookies only with your prior consent. Without consent, non-essential cookies are not set and personalised ads are disabled.\
– California: you may opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising via Your California Privacy Choices.\
We do not use special-category or sensitive data for advertising, and we do not make decisions with legal or similarly significant effects based solely on automated processing.

**What can you do if you don't want cookies to be set or want them to be removed?**

You have the option to disable and delete cookies that may not be necessary for the basic functionality of our website. Please note, blocking categories may impact your experience on our website. You may access the manage Cookies at any time in the footer of our website, or click on your account's setting.

**Cookies on third-party websites (outside our control)**\
When you leave our Sites (for example, to complete a survey or a payment) you may visit third-party websites that set their own cookies. Those cookies are governed by the third party’s policies, not ours, and are not listed in our Cookie Table. Our consent tools apply only to cookies set on our Sites.

**How To Control and Delete Cookies**

1.     **Using Your Browser**

Many of the cookies used on our website and through emails can be enabled or disabled through our consent tool or by disabling the cookies through your browser. To disable cookies through your browser, you may access the manage Cookies at any time in the footer of our website, or click on your account's setting. Please note that disabling a cookie or category of cookies does not delete the cookie from your browser unless manually completed through your browser function.

2.    **Cookies Set in the Past**

Collection of your data from our analytics cookies can be deleted. If cookies are deleted, the information collected prior to the preference change may still be used. However, we will stop using the disabled cookie to collect any further information from your user experience.
